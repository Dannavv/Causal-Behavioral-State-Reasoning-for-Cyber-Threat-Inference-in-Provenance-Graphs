Combining causal reasoning with LLM assistance can therefore
enable IDS that detect not only what happens, but also why it
happens and which interventions are most effective.
In this paper, we propose CausalGraph-IDS, a framework
that integrates causal reasoning with LLM assistance to address
key limitations of current IDS. Rather than asking only whether
an event resembles known attacks, our approach asks a causal
question: Would this alert persist if we intervened to remove
its apparent cause? CausalGraph-IDS builds on an AttackChain Structural Model (ACSM), a temporally ordered causal
representation of multi-stage attack progressions. We introduce
two robustness measures: the Interventional Persistence Index
(IPI) and the Counterfactual Detection Score (CDS), which
quantify whether detected attack chains remain stable under
realistic Security Operations Center (SOC) interventions. To
support deployment, we apply Conformal Risk Control (CRC)
to provide auditable guarantees on false-positive rates without
assuming stationarity. Finally, we use LLMs strictly as assistive
components through three modules: Large Language Modelbased Prior Induction (LLMPI) to extract causal priors from
CTI, SACG to propose realistic counterfactual interventions,
and CLJ to generate human-readable causal explanations.
For confirmed alerts, the system outputs a Minimal BreakIntervention Set (MBIS), i.e., the smallest set of defensive
actions needed to disrupt the inferred attack chain.

Abstract—Modern intrusion detection systems often achieve
impressive benchmark accuracy yet fail in real-world deployment,
where network behavior and attacker tactics continuously evolve.
Under concept drift, decision boundaries learned offline can
diverge from operational reality, triggering false-alarm cascades
and creating detection blind spots that erode analyst trust. In
this paper, we propose CausalGraph-IDS, a causal and languagemodel-assisted intrusion detection framework that moves beyond
purely correlational scoring by explicitly verifying multi-stage
attack chains. Additionally, we propose CHAIN-CRC, a unified
algorithm that (i) learns a constrained attack-chain causal graph
guided by knowledge-based priors derived from widely used
adversary behavior taxonomies, (ii) computes robustness scores
by testing whether alarms persist under feasible counterfactual
security interventions, and (iii) applies conformal risk control to
enforce operator-defined false-positive budgets with finite-sample
guarantees.
Generative models are integrated in strictly assistive roles
through three modules: prior induction to distill causal constraints
from unstructured threat reports, counterfactual generation to
propose realistic and operationally feasible interventions, and
causal logic justification to produce human-readable explanations
grounded in the learned attack chain. Experiments on two
widely used network intrusion detection benchmarks show that
CausalGraph-IDS provides robust, explainable, and risk-governed
detection, maintaining strong recall at low false-positive rates
while delivering actionable causal insights for mitigation.
Index Terms—Intrusion Detection, Causal Reasoning, LLMs,
Conformal Risk Control, Generative AI, Concept Drift.

operating point substantially (TPR@1%FPR=0.9970 vs 0.7866
and 0.9008) and yields better calibration (ECE=0.0028 vs

CICIDS2017 shows a near-saturated operating regime under
our strict 1% FPR constraint. Across several ablation variants,
the negative-score tail becomes extremely concentrated, causing
CRC to select a threshold close to 1.0. When τ ∗ is saturated,
multiple variants can yield identical alert decisions. In contrast,
the full system jointly changes the score distribution through
the combination of ATT&CK-informed priors (LLMPI) and
counterfactual exposure (SACG), which widens separation at
the negative tail and prevents threshold saturation, producing
the observed gains.

B. Discussion: Operational Interpretation
Our results highlight that operating-point control is as
important as raw discrimination. On UNSW-NB15, LLMPI
and SACG improve robustness under strict false-positive
constraints by stabilizing the ACSM structure (ATT&CKaligned priors) and by evaluating persistence under realistic
interventions, which improves TPR@1%FPR. CRC further
enforces a deployment-time risk budget, making the detector
behavior auditable for SOC use.
The dataset CICIDS2017 is highly separable under the
chosen feature representation, and many models reach nearperfect AUROC/AUPRC. At the strict 1% FPR operating point,
CRC calibration can saturate the threshold, and when ablations
share the same persistence gate, the resulting decision policy
can become identical across variants. The Full System uses
the complete pipeline and a tuned persistence gate, shifting
the operating point and recovering additional true positives
while maintaining a very low false-positive rate. Finally, IPI
and CDS play complementary roles: IPI acts as a robustness
gate (filtering superficial correlations), while CDS provides a
smooth robustness score enabling stable ranking and calibration,
particularly when scores concentrate near the threshold.
V. C ONCLUSION
We introduced CausalGraph-IDS, a novel intrusion detection
framework that integrates causal reasoning with LLMs to
address key limitations in traditional IDS. By replacing purely
correlational scoring with causal verification of attack chains,
our framework provides a more robust and interpretable
approach to intrusion detection. Through the introduction of the
IPI and CDS, we enable the framework to assess the stability
of detected attack chains under feasible security interventions.
Additionally, the integration of CRC ensures that the framework

adheres to predefined false-positive budgets, making it suitable
for real-world deployment. Our evaluation results show that
CausalGraph-IDS outperforms state-of-the-art models in terms
of detection performance, interpretability, and risk control.
Future work includes supporting streaming deployment
with periodic CRC recalibration, validating the approach on
live SOC telemetry, extending CHAIN-CRC to multi-stage
Advanced Persistent Threat scenarios, and integrating domainadapted LLMs for higher-quality CTI priors. We will also
study robustness against adaptive attackers targeting the learned
causal structure and investigate integration into SIEM/SOAR
workflows with analyst-in-the-loop evaluation.
